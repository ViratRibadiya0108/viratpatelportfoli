Home Process

A process built for clarity.

Every project I take on runs through the same seven steps. The point isn't to follow a recipe — it's so we both know exactly where we are, what's next, and what's at risk.

01

Discovery

A focused first conversation to understand the business, the users, and the actual goal. Not feature-collecting — problem-mapping.

02

Research & Strategy

Competitive teardown, user-flow mapping, scope definition. We agree on a written brief before anyone touches Figma.

03

UI/UX Design

Wireframes, then a high-fidelity prototype in Figma. We iterate fast — the goal is clarity for engineering, not pixel perfection in isolation.

04

Development

Weekly sprints with demos at the end of each one. You see real, working software almost from the start — no six-month black box.

05

Quality Assurance

Manual QA on real devices, automated test coverage for critical paths, performance audits and edge-case hunting before anything ships.

06

Launch & Deployment

App Store and Play Store submissions, production deploy pipelines, monitoring and alerting set up before launch day — not after.

07

Support & Iteration

Post-launch retainer for bug fixes, performance work and shipping the next round of features. Your product gets to keep growing.

Why work with me

What makes this different.

Eight things I do differently — the kind of things that quietly determine whether a project ships, scales, and survives.

01

Clean architecture

Code structured so the next engineer doesn't have to apologize for it. Modular, tested, documented.

02

Fast communication

Replies within hours, weekly demos, written status updates. You always know where things stand.

03

Built to scale

Architected for the next 10x — not just the demo. Database design, caching, queues, all considered up front.

04

Business-first thinking

I ask why before how. If a feature won't move the metric, we talk about it before I build it.

05

UI/UX excellence

I care about the pixels. Interfaces feel considered, brand-aligned, and like someone gave a damn.

06

Long-term support

I don't disappear at launch. Most clients stay on retainer for the next round of features and fixes.

07

Performance focus

Sub-second load times, smooth 60fps animations, optimized bundles. Speed is a feature.

08

Modern stack

Flutter, Next.js, TypeScript, Node. The current best-of-breed — not whatever I learned in 2018.
